## Distribution Use Cases

| Distribution Type                                                                                            | Use Cases in SCM                                                             | Examples                                                                                                |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Empirical Data Distribution | Modeling real-world phenomena based on historical data                       | \- Customer Demand Patterns <br> - Lead Time Variability <br> - Return Rates                                        |
| Poisson Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_03.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | Events occurring independently at a constant rate                            | \- Arrival of Orders <br> - Supply Chain Failures <br> - Warehouse Receiving Activities                             |
| Uniform Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_02.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | Simplifications when specific patterns are not evident                       | \- Random Inspections <br> - Lead Times for New Suppliers <br> - Task Time Estimations                              |
| Normal Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_01.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | Data clustering around a central value with symmetric tails                  | \- Demand Forecasting <br> - Manufacturing Process Control <br> - Transportation Time Analysis                      |
| Triangle Distribution       | Limited data but known minimum, maximum, and most likely outcomes            | \- Project Completion Times <br> - Cost Estimation for New Products <br> - Supplier Performance Assessment          |
| Exponential Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_04.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | Time between continuous, independent events at a constant rate               | \- Time Between Supply Chain Disruptions <br> - Lead Time Distribution <br> - Failure Rates of Equipment         |
| Log-Normal Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_05.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | Positively skewed data where the log of the variable is normally distributed | \- Product Life Cycle Times <br> - Demand Distribution for High-value Items <br> - Order Quantity Distribution |
| Weibull Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_06.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | Flexible modeling for various behaviors                                      | \- Equipment Lifespan and Reliability <br> - Lead Time Variability <br> - Product Failure Rates                     |
| Beta Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_07.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | Variables with a finite range, such as proportions and percentages           | \- Project Completion Progress <br> - Quality Control Metrics <br> - Inventory Levels as a Proportion of Capacity |
| Bernoulli Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_08.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | Binary outcomes                                                              | \- Quality Inspection Outcomes <br> - Demand Occurrence <br> - Supplier On-time Delivery                            |
| Binomial Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_09.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | | |
| Gamma Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_10.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | | |
| Student t-Distribution <br> <img src="/assets/images/knowledge/solution/dist/dist_11.webp" alt="drawing"/>  | | |
